LEGO and IKEA both make modular products with a heavy focus on design. Why not have them join forces and create a revolutionary interlocking furniture system?

While LEGO and IKEA have previously collaborated on a storage solution, I see far greater opportunity in the overlap of their design philosophies — bringing accessible, modular solutions to the masses.

The Case for Collaboration

The partnership infrastructure already exists. IKEA's history of standout collaborations — most notably the bestselling SONOS speaker series — shows how powerfully they can expand a shared vision when the brand alignment is right.

Both companies share a core belief: that good design should be accessible. LEGO's interlocking system applied to furniture scale opens up a category that neither brand could own alone.
