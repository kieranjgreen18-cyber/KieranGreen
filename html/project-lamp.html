01 / PH Lamp — Physical Model Laser Cut Cardboard

The best ideas don't always survive contact with reality. Sometimes what replaces them is more interesting.

The original concept was a spinning PH lamp — a kinetic take on Henningsen's iconic layered form. It didn't work. The rotation created problems I couldn't resolve within the project's constraints, so I pivoted: instead of animating the whole lamp, I turned my attention to the bulb itself. What would it mean to wrap the light source directly — to make the shade not a shell around the lamp, but a collar around the glow?

The Process

I worked through the idea in hand sketches, exploring how shade geometry changes when the reference point is the bulb rather than a fixture above it. Each iteration went straight to the laser cutter — cardboard kept the feedback loop fast. The assembly was hand-built, letting me test how light actually behaved through each form rather than guessing from a drawing.

What This Demonstrates

This project didn't go to plan — and that's the point. Recognising when an idea isn't working, making a deliberate pivot, and finding something genuinely worth pursuing in the wreckage: that's the design process at its most real. It sharpened my ability to iterate fast, stay curious under constraint, and treat failure as a prompt rather than a dead end.

The pivot is where the
real idea begins.
