About

B.Sc Industrial Design

Not just beautiful. Better.

I'm an industrial design student who came to this field through living it. The tools that rebuilt a car engine. The bike components I put in strangers' hands at Mike's Bikes. The guitar that taught me what rightness feels like before it sounds like anything. I'm studying to turn that into a skill I can give to other people.

KG
01

The Engine Bay

Rebuilt a car.
Learned what
constraints feel like.

Hands deep in an engine block, nothing is arbitrary. Every surface finish, every tolerance, every fastener is a decision with a consequence you can feel. Good design is engineering made honest and inevitable.

02

The Shop Floor

Sold bikes.
Learned what
users actually need.

At Mike's Bikes I watched real people touch real components and make real decisions. A spec sheet means nothing at that moment. The gap between what a designer intends and what a user experiences — that's where I want to work.

03

The Practice Room

Play guitar.
Chase that
intangible rightness.

A great guitar has a quality you feel before you hear it — weight, balance, resonance in the body. That thing that can't be specced but is instantly felt. That's what I'm designing toward.

About Kieran Green

Kieran Green is an industrial designer currently studying Industrial Design. He specialises in CAD modeling, surface development, FDM prototyping, and product visualization. He is seeking internships, freelance projects, and collaborations.

Skills and Software

  • Rhino 3D — surface modeling, SubD modeling, CAD modeling
  • Keyshot — product visualization and rendering
  • Premiere Pro — motion and animation direction
  • FDM Prototyping — physical model production
  • Form Development — ideation through manufacture-ready geometry

Selected Projects

  • 3D Printed Car (2025): Surface modeled a Mini Clubman in Rhino 3D and produced it as an FDM 3D print. Tools: Rhino 3D, FDM.
  • Sabretta Pen (2025): Ballpoint pen design with form inspired by 1930s roadster fenders. Fully modeled, animated and directed. Tools: Rhino 3D, Keyshot, Premiere Pro.
  • Poul Henningsen Lamp (2025): CAD recreation of the iconic PH lamp, studying light diffusion through geometric form. Tools: Rhino 3D.
  • Intro to Rhino (2025): Headphones, power drill, and Minion helmet modeled across four weeks of Rhino 3D fundamentals. Tools: Rhino 3D, Keyshot, SubD.
