Personal Statement — 2025 Kieran Green Why I design

About

B.Sc Industrial Design

Not just beautiful. Better.

I'm an industrial design student who came to this field through living it. The tools that rebuilt a car engine. The bike components I put in strangers' hands at Mike's Bikes. The guitar that taught me what rightness feels like before it sounds like anything. I'm studying to turn that into a skill I can give to other people.

KG
01

The Engine Bay

Rebuilt a car.
Learned what
constraints feel like.

Hands deep in an engine block, nothing is arbitrary. Every surface finish, every tolerance, every fastener is a decision with a consequence you can feel. Good design is engineering made honest and inevitable.

02

The Shop Floor

Sold bikes.
Learned what
users actually need.

At Mike's Bikes I watched real people touch real components and make real decisions. A spec sheet means nothing at that moment. The gap between what a designer intends and what a user experiences — that's where I want to work.

03

The Practice Room

Play guitar.
Chase that
intangible rightness.

A great guitar has a quality you feel before you hear it — weight, balance, resonance in the body. That thing that can't be specced but is instantly felt. That's what I'm designing toward.
