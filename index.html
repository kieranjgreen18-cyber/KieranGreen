Hi, I'm Kieran Green.

Industrial Design student at SCAD — based in the San Francisco region. I work where craft meets engineering: from sketches to surfaces you can hold.

01 — Origin

Rebuilt a car. Learned what constraints feel like.

Hands deep in an engine block, nothing is arbitrary. Every surface finish, every tolerance, every fastener is a decision with a consequence you can feel. Good design is engineering made honest and inevitable.

02 — Experience

Sold bikes. Learned what users actually need.

At Mike's Bikes I watched real people touch real components and make real decisions. A spec sheet means nothing at that moment. The gap between what a designer intends and what a user experiences — that's where I want to work.

03 — Instinct

03 — Instinct

Built a chair. Learned how form and engineering work together.

With no adhesives or fasteners allowed, you learn how continuity is strong. How form is strength — when your design literally carries weight. Good design is more than just eye candy — it lifts you up.

About Kieran Green

Kieran Green is an industrial designer specialising in CAD modeling, surface development, FDM prototyping, and product visualization. Seeking internships, freelance projects, and collaborations.

Skills and Software

  • Rhino 3D — surface modeling, SubD modeling, CAD modeling
  • Keyshot — product visualization and rendering
  • Premiere Pro — motion and animation direction
  • FDM Prototyping — physical model production
  • Form Development — ideation through manufacture-ready geometry
