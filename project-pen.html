What if a ballpoint pen could be sexy? That was the question I set out to answer with the Sabretta — direct modeled in Rhino 3D, animated in Keyshot, then taken into Premiere Pro.

Using my experience producing documentaries, I created an ad inspired by how perfume advertisements sell on emotion rather than function. The result treats an everyday object with the seriousness it deserves.

The Film

This was my first time animating in Keyshot, and I ran into significant challenges — the program was crashing repeatedly during render. Fortunately, I was able to take the individual animated frames directly into Premiere Pro, speed them up by a factor of 10,000, and still achieve a smooth, composed viewing experience.

The stock footage is from Pexels and is free use. Every other element — modeling, lighting, rendering, directing, editing — is entirely my own work.

The Form

The form of this pen was inspired by the pointed, flowing fenders of 1930s roadsters, in particular the Delahaye 165. The internals are fully modeled — if adjusted to accommodate an off-the-shelf ink cartridge, it could be 3D printed and would function as a working pen.

This wasn't a pen designed for productivity. It was designed to be held.
