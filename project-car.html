Subject

Mini Clubman

I surface modeled my personal vehicle — a Mini Clubman — in Rhino 3D, then 3D printed it in FDM plastic.

The final print wasn't perfect by any means. But there is nothing cooler than getting to hold something that was the product of your own imagination and many hours of blood, sweat and tears.

The Challenge

My biggest challenge was achieving a closed object out of my surfaces. To get something printable, I had to sacrifice a few details I'd added — the exhaust pipes and the brake cooling duct behind the rear wheel. Real objects have constraints, and that's exactly where the interesting design problem lives.

Process

Making a rough outline of the shape first — this basic structure was achieved by generating a series of curves from two reference lines. Over the course of building this model, most of those surfaces were redone as I discovered easier and better ways to accomplish the surface layouts for the shapes I'd intended.

Every iteration was a lesson in how Rhino handles surface continuity. The discipline of working toward a watertight, closed mesh changed how I think about modeling entirely — detail is secondary to structure.
