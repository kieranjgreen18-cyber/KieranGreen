I surface modeled my personal vehicle — a Mini Clubman — in Rhino 3D, then held the result in my hands.

The final 3D print wasn't perfect by any means, but there is nothing cooler than holding something that was the product of your own imagination and many hours of blood, sweat, and tears.

The Challenge

My biggest challenge was achieving a closed object out of my surfaces. To get something printable I had to sacrifice a few details — the exhaust pipes and the brake cooling duct behind the rear wheel.

Making a rough outline of the shape, this basic structure was achieved by making a bunch of curves from two lines. Over the process of building this model, most of these surfaces were redone as I discovered easier and better ways to accomplish the layouts I had intended.
